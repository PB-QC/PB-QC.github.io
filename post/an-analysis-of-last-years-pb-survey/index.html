An analysis of last year's PB survey

Introduction

       This report is a preliminary analysis of the Queens College survey data. The report is divided into the following sections I. Overview of data and concerns II. Prelimnary summary statistics III. Possible Future explorations.

I. Data Overview

       As with most surveys, the data is fairly messy in particular there are lots of missing values across the varibales and there are various ways that can be dealt with. I disscus some of the problematic variables below and show some tables.

A. Correlation Plot

This plot shows which variables correlate strongly or not at all with each other. The deeper the blue, the stronger the positive correlation. Deeper red indicates negative correlation.

II. Summary Stats

      The Tables below present summary statistics of the quantative variables for all students and various subgroups. Note that diet is 1 if a person has any sort of dietary restrictions and 0 if they don’t, and gender is coded 1 for male 0 for female with any gender nonconforming excluded.Current status2 1 if full time 0 for part time.

      Asians and Hispanics study at midnight more at 73% while whites lowest at 57%. Somewhat surprised not big differences in food variables by dietary restrictions although Vegans do rate a .3-.5 lower than pop average for food health, quality and variety. Slight differnces in male v female rating of campus safety by almost .2 but not huge
